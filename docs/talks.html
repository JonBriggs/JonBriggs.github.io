Speaking & advising

Advising schools through the evolution of instruction.

I work with a small number of schools, leadership teams, and boards on the strategic, instructional, and ethical implications of AI — not to hand over answers, but to help a community find its own.

How I work with schools

Common areas of focus

Most of the schools I talk with aren't looking for a vendor or a verdict. They're looking for a way to think clearly about a fast-moving subject without losing sight of what they already know about good teaching. That's the work: helping a community engage AI on its own terms, grounded in its own mission.

01

Preparing for the board conversation

Helping Heads frame AI for governance — what trustees need to understand, and what they should be asking — before strategy gets set.

02

Strategy anchored in mission

Framing AI decisions around learning and institutional values rather than features, so adoption strengthens the school instead of distorting it.

03

Faculty readiness without hype or panic

Supporting teachers through real change — taking their concerns seriously while keeping momentum and curiosity alive.

04

Clear expectations for AI use

Helping schools articulate what responsible student and teacher use actually looks like, in language a community can live by.

05

Right-sized next steps

Translating big questions into practical, achievable moves — the difference between a plan and a press release.

06

Tools that fit the work

Where it helps, designing or evaluating the actual software — drawing on the systems I've built and run in a real school.
