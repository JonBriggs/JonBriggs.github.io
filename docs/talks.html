Talks & Advising

I work with a small number of schools, leadership teams, and boards on the implications of AI for teaching and learning — not to hand over answers, but to help a community find its own.

Most schools I talk with aren't looking for a vendor or a verdict. They're looking for a way to think clearly about a fast-moving subject without losing what they already know about good teaching. That's the work — and because I do it from inside a school, with my hands on the actual technology, it stays grounded in practice rather than slideware.

Common areas of focus

Preparing for the board conversation

Helping heads frame AI for governance — what trustees need to understand, and what they should be asking — before strategy gets set.

Strategy anchored in mission

Framing AI decisions around learning and institutional values rather than features, so adoption strengthens the school instead of distorting it.

Faculty readiness without hype or panic

Taking teachers' concerns seriously while keeping curiosity and momentum alive.

Clear expectations for AI use

Helping a community articulate what responsible student and teacher use actually looks like, in language it can live by.

For a speaking, advising, or school-leadership conversation, write to jonathan.briggs@gmail.com with your school, your role, and what you're hoping to work through.
