About

An educator and technologist working at the edge of instruction.

I'm Director of Technology at Eastside Preparatory School — a builder who thinks, and a thinker who ships.

I work on how artificial intelligence is changing teaching, learning, and institutional leadership. My work rests on a simple belief: AI hasn't changed the purpose of schools, but it has made clarity more important. We have to be able to name what good learning looks like, where effort matters, how judgment develops, and how a tool should serve human growth rather than quietly replace it.

What makes that more than commentary, I hope, is that I do it from inside a school and with my hands on the actual technology. I write and advise on these questions, and I also build the software that tests whether the ideas hold up — placement models, classroom tools, data pipelines that answer questions schools genuinely have. Theory illuminates practice; it never gets to exist for its own sake.

How I think about technology in schools

The reality of AI in education is not as intimidating as we sometimes imagine, but it isn't trivial either. There's a distinction to be made between tools that build a student's capacity and tools that quietly become crutches — and only the person doing the learning really knows which is which. That's the kind of problem I find worth the effort: not because there's a clean answer, but because working it out together is how a school stays itself while the ground shifts underneath.
